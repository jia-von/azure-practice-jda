Exam AZ-900: Microsoft Azure Fundamentals	"Describe Redundancy in the <span style=""color: rgb(0, 170, 255);""><b>Primary Region</b></span>"	"<span style=""color: rgb(0, 170, 255);""><b>Locally redundant storage&nbsp;</b></span>(LRS)<br><img src=""https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/wwl-azure/describe-azure-storage-services/media/locally-redundant-storage-37247957.png""><br>LRS replicates your data three times within a single data center in the primary region.<br><ul><li>LRS is the lowest-cost redundancy option and offers the least durability compared to other options. LRS protects your data against server rack and drive failures.&nbsp;</li><li>However, if a disaster such as fire or flooding occurs within the data center, all replicas of a storage account using LRS may be lost or unrecoverable.</li></ul><span style=""color: rgb(0, 170, 255);""><b>Zone-redundant storage&nbsp;</b></span>(ZRS)<br><img src=""https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/wwl-azure/describe-azure-storage-services/media/zone-redundant-storage-6dd46d22.png""><br><ul><li>For Availability Zone-enabled Regions, ZRS replicates your Azure Storage data synchronously across three Azure availability zones in the primary region.&nbsp;</li><li>With ZRS, your data is still accessible for both read and write 
operations even if a zone becomes unavailable. No remounting of Azure 
file shares from the connected clients is required.&nbsp;</li><li>If a zone becomes 
unavailable, Azure undertakes networking updates, such as DNS 
repointing. These updates may affect your application if you access data
 before the updates have completed.<br></li></ul>"	2.3
Exam AZ-900: Microsoft Azure Fundamentals	Describe Redundancy in a Secondary Region	"Geo-redundant storage (with GRS or GZRS) replicates your data to another physical location in the secondary region to protect against regional outages. However, that data is available to be read only if the customer or Microsoft initiates a failover from the primary to secondary region<br><b><span style=""color: rgb(0, 170, 255);""><br>Geo-redundant storage<br></span></b><img src=""https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/wwl-azure/describe-azure-storage-services/media/geo-redundant-storage-3432d558.png""><br><ul><li>GRS is similar to running LRS in two regions<br></li><li>GRS copies your data synchronously three times within a single physical location in the primary region using LRS<br></li><li>It then copies your data asynchronously to a single physical location in the secondary region (the region pair) using LRS<br></li></ul><span style=""color: rgb(85, 170, 255);""><b>Geo-zone-redundant storage<br></b></span><img src=""https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/wwl-azure/describe-azure-storage-services/media/geo-zone-redundant-storage-138ab5af.png""><br><ul><li>GZRS is similar to running ZRS in the primary region and LRS in the secondary region</li><li>GZRS combines the high availability provided by redundancy across availability zones with protection from regional outages provided by geo-replication.&nbsp;</li><li>Data in a GZRS storage account is copied across three Azure availability zones in the primary region (similar to ZRS) and is also replicated to a secondary geographic region, using LRS, for protection from regional disasters.<br></li></ul>"	2.3
